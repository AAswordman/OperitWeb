Operit is an all-in-one mobile assistant that can integrate with system capabilities to handle lots of tasks across daily use, office workflows, and technical scenarios.

I believe many people’s first reaction when they open Operit is:

What is this thing, and why are there so many buttons?

No rush — in the following docs, we’ll walk through the software configuration step by step.

At its core, this app is still about conversation. Learning how to communicate with AI is a very important skill. Since current AI still isn’t smart enough in many cases, it can easily misunderstand intent. So we should try to reduce ambiguity and make our goals as clear as possible. If you think about how people talk to each other, isn’t this the same communication problem?

The core logic is simple: how you talk to AI and trigger other tools. After installing the app, many things are already auto-configured. What you really need to do is simple: grant permissions, fill in a key, and start chatting.

Then a question naturally comes up: Why do I still need to authorize more stuff after granting permissions? What is this key, and why can’t I just start chatting directly?

Yeah, here’s a simple way to understand it. The app works at a pretty low level, so some restricted capabilities — like developer-level or root-related permissions — require extra authorization.

As for the key, just follow the guide and go to the DeepSeek page to apply and top up. (We have no partnership with DeepSeek — we only use it as an example because its website is easy to understand and the model quality is decent.)

The detailed parts can get a bit complex, but this documentation will do its best to explain everything clearly.
